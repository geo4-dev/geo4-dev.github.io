Training Modules

h1>
div>
header>

The Geo4Dev initiative is offering a suite of learning modules aimed at training interested researchers and analysts to use novel geographic datasets and methods. The modules are formed around accessible, research-ready tutorials which walk users through accessing, handling, and analyzing novel or technical geographic data sources.

p>

The Geo4Dev tutorials, and other training resources, are created to ensure geographic data sources are not limited only to those with technical backgrounds, but instead truly fulfill their open-source promise and potential.

If you are interested in developing a learning module for Geo4Dev, please contact info@geo4.dev.

div>
section>

Satellite Crop Type Mapping

This tutorial provides guidance on creating a machine learning model to identify crop types from satellite imagery and other earth observation data. For users with a sample of known crop locations, the tutorial demonstrates the steps required to train a model on the known samples and predict crop locations for a wider area of interest.

Get starteda>

Applications of Remote Sensing for Impact Evaluation

Geospatial data can play a significant and increasing role in supporting impact evaluations as well as other stages of the policy process such as design and implementation. Newly available sources can give us great insight into issues such as the changing coverage of forests, vegetation, crops, water, air quality, buildings and infrastructure.

p>
Get starteda>

Population Weighted Wealth

This Geo4Dev tutorial guides users through creation of population-weighted wealth maps and accompanying statistics for administrative areas of interest, the product of a collaboration between the Center for Effective Global Action (CEGA) and Facebook's Data for Good.

Get starteda>

Radiance Calibrated Nighttime Tutorials

This tutorial draws from Marco Gonzalez-Navarro and Matthew Turner's use of Radiance Calibrated Nighttime Lights in their paper on subways and urban growth, which focuses on analysis of data from sensors calibrated to avoid saturation of urban centers.

Get starteda>

Global Subway Systems

This Geo4Dev tutorial makes utilizing novel data from research on subways and urban growth accessible to all audiences regardless of technical ability, demonstrating all code required to import subway data, select locations, create summary statistics, and build maps and graphical visualizations.

Get starteda>

Deforestation and Land Cover

Information about vegetation coverage of the earth's surface, particularly changes in coverage, enables the study of questions in fields ranging from the natural to the social sciences. This tutorial provides an approachable introduction to the MODIS Vegetation Continuous Fields (VCF) product.

Get starteda>

Open Nighttime Lights

Intro to Nighttime Lights and the Google Earth Engine API.

Visit the ONL training sitea>

Crop Yield Mapping Using Satellite Data & Land Cover

This tutorial provides guidance on creating a machine learning model to predict maize yields from satellite imagery and other earth observation data. We train on variety trial data collected by CIMMYT.

Get starteda>

Woody Cover Estimation

This tutorial provides guidance on creating a predictive modeling workflow to quantify woody cover from remote sensing datasets (Airborne Light Detection and Ranging (LiDAR) and Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR)).
