ABOUT

About Geo4Dev

The Geospatial Analysis for Development Initiative makes geospatial data and tools accessible to researchers, analysts, and policymakers working in international development.

OUR MISSION

Empowering Development Through Geospatial Data

The Geo4Dev Initiative is run in collaboration between New Light Technologies Inc., the Center for Effective Global Action (CEGA) at UC Berkeley, and 3IE (International Initiative for Impact Evaluation).

Our mission is to ensure geographic data sources are not limited only to those with technical backgrounds, but instead truly fulfill their open-source promise and potential. We provide accessible, research-ready tutorials and tools for researchers and practitioners worldwide.

Key Goals
Make geospatial data accessible to all
Bridge technical barriers for researchers
Support evidence-based policy decisions
Train analysts in LMICs
Build open-source geospatial tools

OBJECTIVES

What We Do

Research Publications

Curate timely and relevant journals, datasets, and publications on geospatial data for development.

Training Modules

Develop accessible learning modules that walk users through novel geographic datasets and methods.

Interactive Tools

Build mapping tools and data browsers for exploring geotagged research and datasets.

Conferences

Host symposiums bringing together researchers, policymakers, and practitioners in geospatial analysis.

Community Building

Connect researchers and analysts across institutions working with geospatial data for development.

Open Data Access

Promote open access to geospatial datasets for international development research.
